Tasks

You will be tested on four different tasks which will measure separate aspects of spatial ability. The four tests are:

  1. Mental Rotation Task
  2. Placing Buildings on a Map
  3. Tracing Routes from Stories
  4. Identifying Locations in Pictures

Before each test, there will be an instruction screen that will give you background information, including how to input your answers. When you have read the instructions, continue to the next screen where you will complete the test.

Instructions for the Mental Rotation Task

In this test, you will be given two images, such as the ones shown below. Your task is to determine whether or not the images are simply rotated, or whether they are flipped and rotated.

If you believe that the images are Flipped, press F. If you believe that the images are Just rotated, press J. In the example above, you would press J, because the shapes are just rotations of one another.

There will be 30 trials. You will have the ability to view your results at the end of the survey, after all tasks are complete.

Mental Roation Task

Press F is the image is flipped, J if it is just rotated.

Instructions for Placing Buildings on a Map

In this test, you will be presented with a blank map of campus that includes only the roads. You will have a set of buildings to place on a map. Your task is to determine where the buildings will go.

Buildings will be presented one at a time. Click to place the building on the map. Press R to rotate the building 45 degrees.

There will be 15 buildings to place on the map. You will have the ability to view your results at the end of the survey, after all tasks are complete.

Placing Buildings on a Map

Click on the map to place buildings. Press R to rotate.

Instructions for Tracing Routes from Stories

In this test, you will read a short description of an Ole's day. Read the story carefully, and try to visualize where the student went. Once you finish reading the story, you will be presented with a complete map of campus. Your task is to reconstruct the route that you read in the story.

To trace the route on the map, move the mouse to the start of the route, then click the mouse button to create a marker. Then move the mouse to the next location in the story and click to create another marker. A line will indicate that the two markers are connected in the route.

For example, if the story was "I woke up in Mohn, then went to Buntrock for breakfase, then went to Regents for class", then you would first click on Mohn, then on Buntrock, then on Regents (as detailed in the picture above).

There will be 5 stories to read and trace. You will have the ability to view your results at the end of the survey, after all tasks are complete.

Tracing Routes from Stories

Read the story and then click Trace Route to begin tracing.

Click on the map to make a marker, then click at another location to make the route. Click clear to start over.
